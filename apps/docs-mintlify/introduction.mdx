## Why Polpo?

AI agents are great at writing code. They're terrible at finishing complex tasks and projects. One agent hallucinates, another breaks what the first one built, a third silently hangs. You end up babysitting the machines that were supposed to save you time — 4 monitors, 12 terminals open, and zero confidence that anything actually works.

<p><span style={{ fontWeight: 700 }}>Polpo fixes this.</span> You talk to Polpo like you'd talk to a project manager — describe what you need, and it assembles the right agents, plans the work, checks every result, retries what's broken, and escalates what it can't fix. You define the goal, Polpo makes sure it actually gets done.</p>

<p><span style={{ fontWeight: 700 }}>Who is it for?</span> Anyone who wants a personal AI team on their laptop, a $5 VPS, or a Docker container in their cloud. Tell Polpo to build an app, run a virtual AI company, or automate your workflows — and it handles the rest. One <code>npm install</code> and you're chatting. No hosted platform, no vendor lock-in — your hardware, your API keys, your rules.</p>

<p><span style={{ fontWeight: 700 }}>Prerequisites.</span> Node 22+ and an API key or subscription from any of the 22+ supported providers — OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Mistral, Ollama, you name it.</p>

## What's different

- <span style={{ fontWeight: 700 }}>Chat or automate.</span> Tell Polpo what you need in plain language, or define repeatable plans as JSON. Same gates, same team, same results — every time.
- <span style={{ fontWeight: 700 }}>Reliable output, not best-effort.</span> Polpo scores every task with LLM judges. Below threshold? It sends the agent back to fix it. You get the result, not the retries.
- <span style={{ fontWeight: 700 }}>Run it and walk away.</span> Start a plan, close your laptop, come back to results. Polpo and its agents survive crashes and restarts on their own.
- <span style={{ fontWeight: 700 }}>Proactive, not passive.</span> Polpo reaches out to you — Slack, email, Telegram, webhooks — only when it matters.
